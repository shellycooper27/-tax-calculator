Income from SalaryHead 1

Standard deduction is applied automatically (Old ₹50,000 / New ₹75,000). HRA exemption u/s 10(13A) is allowed only under the Old Regime.

Income from House PropertyHead 2

Self-occupied interest u/s 24(b) is capped at ₹2,00,000 and allowed under the Old Regime only. Loss set-off against other heads is restricted to ₹2,00,000.

Profits & Gains of Business / ProfessionHead 3

Enter either the regular computation figures or presumptive income (44AD / 44ADA / 44AE). Presumptive income, where entered, is taken as the business income.

Capital GainsHead 4

Special-rate gains are taxed separately: STCG u/s 111A @20%, LTCG u/s 112A @12.5% (exemption ₹1,25,000), LTCG u/s 112 @12.5%. For residents, unexhausted basic exemption is set off against special-rate income.

Income from Other SourcesHead 5

Lottery / online gaming / VDA / unexplained income are taxed at special rates (30% / 30% / 30% / 60% respectively) and are not eligible for rebate u/s 87A.

Agricultural IncomePartial Integration

Net agricultural income above ₹5,000 is aggregated for rate purposes (partial integration) where the taxpayer is an individual/HUF and non-agricultural income exceeds the basic exemption.

Deductions — Chapter VI-AOld Regime

These deductions are available under the Old Regime. Statutory ceilings (e.g. 80C aggregate ₹1,50,000; 80CCD(1B) ₹50,000) are applied automatically, with a validation note.

DeductionsNew Regime

Only a limited set of deductions is permitted under the New Regime u/s 115BAC.

Interest u/s 234B & 234CDefault Interest

How to Use This Calculator
  1. Select the Financial Year, Taxpayer Category (age), Taxpayer Type, and the regime(s) you want to compare on the Tax Calculator tab.
  2. Expand each income head (Salary, House Property, Business/Profession, Capital Gains, Other Sources) and fill in the relevant figures. Leave anything not applicable at 0.
  3. Under the Old Regime, fill in Chapter VI-A deductions (80C, 80D, etc.) — these are ignored automatically under the New Regime.
  4. Enter any taxes already paid (TDS, TCS, advance tax, self-assessment tax) and relief u/s 89 in the last section.
  5. Click Calculate Tax — the sticky summary panel on the right shows the Old vs New regime totals, which one is recommended, and the estimated saving.
  6. Use Load Sample to see a worked example, Save / Load Saved to keep figures for a client between visits, and Export CSV / Download PDF / Print to share the computation.
  7. Use the Advance Tax and Interest 234B/234C tabs to check instalment compliance and any default interest once the annual tax liability is known.
